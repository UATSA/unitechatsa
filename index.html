Message from 2025 Unitech ASA President and Patron.

Elliuda Kaski, 2025 President

Greetings, precious jewel of God. As the president for this term by the grace of God, I welcome you by the love and grace of our Lord Jesus Christ. This is the official website of the Unitech Adventist Tertiary Students Association (Unitech ATSA)- a subsidiary to the Papua New Guinea Adventist Tertiary Students Association (PNG ATSA). It is the Lord’s leading that you were brought to this website and it is my prayer that may you be inspired and blessed to live for the glory of God as you explore what Unitech ATSA is doing for the salvation of God’s people, as a student ministry under Public Campus Ministry (PCM) division of the General Conference of the Seventh Day Adventist Church.
The existence of Unitech ATSA in Papua New Guinea University of Technology shares the same objectives to that of the Papua New Guinea Adventist Tertiary Students Association (PNG ATSA). The main objectives after PCM is to ensure students spiritual, physical and emotional, and intellectual needs are taken cared of so they find a sense of belonging in a secular government institution. There are targeted activities planned in its yearly planner to ensure the objective of human integral development is achieved. Unitech Atsa organized events include spiritual activities such as hospital and prison visitation, conducting branch sabbath schools or Sabbath morning worship with non-Adventists sharing the light of the word of God. Other activities include fundraisings, social games and planned visits to other centers like disability homes and schools.
More importantly, Unitech ATSA exists to bring together young people who are passionate about spreading the Gospel of Christ to the wider Unitech community, in doing so, members who are less active can become more connected to Christ in preparation for His soon return. In addition, Unitech ATSA fosters an environment conducive for spiritual and professional growth. Students involvement helps build their leadership skills, communication, team work and more importantly building characters for the Kingdom of God. These are blessings that come as a result of involvement in the greater work of the Lord. Thank you for visiting. May you continue to grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord Jesus until he comes again.

MARANATHA!

Elder Vincent Kuma, 2025 Patron

In the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, I would like to give a warm welcome to our church families and continuing students for returning back to 2025 academic year. Also a word of congratulations to the first year to Unitech. As the president, I also would like to welcome you to the Adventist Students Association.
