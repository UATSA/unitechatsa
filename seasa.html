President

The President of the Sepik Adventist Students Association (SEASA) is responsible for leading the association with a focus on fostering spiritual growth and unity among students. This role involves guiding SEASA's activities, ensuring they align with the mission of the association, and representing the students within the broader Adventist community. The President works closely with other leaders to plan and organize events that encourage faith, fellowship, and personal development. The leadership of the President is crucial in creating an environment where students can grow in their faith and support one another.

Vice-President

The Vice President of SEASA supports the President in carrying out the association's mission and goals. This role includes helping to plan, organize, and manage events that engage students and promote spiritual growth. The Vice President also steps in to assist the President as needed and helps ensure the smooth operation of SEASA’s activities. With a focus on service and community, the Vice President plays an important role in maintaining a welcoming and supportive environment for all students involved in SEASA.

Spiritual Leader

The Spiritual Leader is the cornerstone of SEASA’s faith journey. This individual guides the spiritual growth of the members through prayer, Bible study, and regular worship services. They ensure that all activities are rooted in the Word of God and provide spiritual counsel and mentorship to members, fostering a deep and personal connection with Christ.

Assistant Spiritual Leader

Treasurer

The Treasurer manages the financial aspect of SEASA, ensuring that funds are allocated responsibly for spiritual growth activities such as retreats, worship services, and charity events. They help ensure that resources are used to support the faith-based mission of the association, facilitating activities that enhance members’ spiritual development and community service.

Secretary

The Secretary is responsible for keeping accurate records of SEASA's spiritual meetings, events, and communications. This role ensures that all important spiritual messages, decisions, and updates are documented and communicated to members. The Secretary helps maintain a smooth flow of information, ensuring that spiritual initiatives and prayer requests are clearly communicated.

Choir Master

The Choir Master leads the SEASA choir in praise and worship, helping to elevate the spiritual atmosphere during services and events. This role is essential in using music as a tool for spiritual expression and worship, encouraging members to offer their talents in service to God through song.

Fundraising

The Fundraising Leader is responsible for organizing fundraising initiatives that support SEASA’s spiritual and community outreach activities. By ensuring that financial resources are available, this role enables the association to carry out its mission of spiritual growth, outreach, and service. Fundraisers are designed to reflect Christian stewardship and support the needs of the wider community.

Assistant Fundraising

Assisting the Fundraising Leader, the Associate Fundraising Leader helps in planning and executing fundraising events that fund SEASA’s spiritual programs and outreach efforts. They contribute to ensuring that resources are raised with integrity, purpose, and an emphasis on supporting faith-based activities.

Social Leader

The Social Leader is responsible for organizing social events that promote unity and bonding within SEASA. They plan activities such as group outings, team-building exercises, and informal gatherings that create a sense of community among members. By fostering a friendly, supportive environment, the Social Leader helps to build strong relationships, which in turn enables members to grow spiritually together as they share experiences and connect on a deeper level.

Assistant Social Leader

The Associate Social Leader supports the Social Leader in planning and coordinating events that allow members to engage in fun and meaningful activities. Their role focuses on ensuring that social gatherings help strengthen the friendships and networks within the association, creating a strong community where members feel connected. These social interactions serve as a foundation for spiritual growth, as they encourage members to support one another and develop a sense of belonging in the SEASA family.

Campus Witnessing

The mission field of the ASA is the campus. Here in the campus, ASA visit students during branch Sabbath School to share the gospel of Jesus Christ. Car park outreach is also major witnessing activity.
